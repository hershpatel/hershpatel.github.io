Pratigna

Pramukh Swami's Pratigna

"On this auspicious day, I pray from my heart to Guruhari, that whatever strength, knowledge, inspiration I have has been given by you. Until now my life as a renunciate has passed in your compassionate service. Now, you have commanded me to serve in this tremendous seva, for which grant me fully the capability and strength. And may also all your disciples be happy in mind, body and wealth; let them be unassailed by any unhappiness or hardship in this world and worshipping Shriji Maharaj at the last, reside in Akshardham. I ask blessings for this. Also, if even unknowingly someone utters the name of Akshar Purushottam Maharaj, grant him Akshardham through your miraculous greatness.”

"Today, before you, Gurushri, and before this assembly I take an oath that I will fully carry out my duties to this Sanstha of yours, without caring for this body, I will remain sincere and be fulfilled. You have cultivated this garden - knowledge of Akshar Purushottam – every day I will persevere and care that it flowers in every way; I will serve, and wish for the blessings of the entire fellowship, grant me this. I ask that in all my duties for the good of Satsang, Pujya Yogi Maharaj, sadgurus, senior sadhus, parshads and all disciples I appeal to with pure heart that all of you give me your full co-operation, so I may be strengthened to perform this tremendous service.”



The Purpose

The theme is Pratigna - how we can keep the promises and commitments we make to ourselves, one another, and our guru. We find it difficult to stay loyal, keep our promises, and keep doing good. It is hard not to rest on our laurels, compare ourselves to others, or believe that others will do it when we cannot. But our guru parampara have kept their pratigna until their last breath, and beyond.


Prarthana

Our pratigna is one of many priorities in the business of our lives. We are geniuses at inventing reasons to avoid change; it is a form of self-protection. This is because our inner beliefs trigger failure before failure even happens. It’s easier to attack a strategy than to solve the problem. Prarthna allows us to see opportunities to challenge those beliefs, and gives the right beliefs a fighting chance. Prarthna needs to be sincere and honest. Sincerity requires effort to understand intent/ruchi. Making a pratigna is a privilege earned by abhyas of ruchi. Prarthna reminds us of intent -- to leave the jersey in a better place. Ritualized prarthna allows us to feel the beliefs. To fix the prarthna, we have to fix our beliefs.


Samarpan

To keep your word, you have to make physical and mental space in your life. But you cannot do that if your environment controls you. You have to be able to access your environment - some environments elevate us, others erase the good vibes. Otherwise we just pick up stock emotions, like stock photos, feeling we have no choice. Making space requires making decisions. To decide means “to cut away”.


Purusharth

We do samarpan to do purusharth. Purusharth shouldn’t be for a reward. Purusharth is effort toward building a value-based, purpose-driven culture – sincere effort toward moksha. Keep doing good; forget about the rest.
